Sustainable Agriculture



Risks

Not using sustainable agricultural techniques or unsustainable agriculture can pose a great risk to human health. Some unsustainable practices can reduce the yield and nutritional value of the crops. Non-organic fertilizers and high-risk pesticides are an example that contaminates the soil of the crops.

There are many problems we can cause to the world if we do not implement these solutions. A few risks include:

  • Soil Degradation: Farming too much can ruin good land for growing
  • Water Scarcity: Bad water use can drain our fresh water supplies.
  • Loss of Biodiversity: Growing just one type of crop and using chemicals can hurt ecosystems.
  • Food Insecurity: Using up resources might cause food shortages.
  • Increased Climate Impact: Regular farming methods add to greenhouse gas emissions.
